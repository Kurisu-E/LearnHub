caedere Latin verb
3rd-conjugation caedō; caedere; cecīdī; caesum
1

: to cut, hew, fell → become descendants like -cide, -cise, or -cis-

abscise
"to cut off or away, remove"

excise/excide(—excision)
"to cut out of or from"

circumcise(—circumcision)
"to cut round or off (the foreskin)"

concise(—concision)
"(figuratively) marked by cutting (expression or statement minutely) and gathering (details)"

decide(—decision—decisive)
"(figuratively) to cut (choices or possibilities) off or down (to one choice or possibility)"

precise(—precision—precisive)
"(figuratively) to cut off (something unnecessary) beforehand to a very high degree"

, also caes- or ces-

caesura
"(figuratively) a function of cutting (in verses)"

*caesarean/cesarean section
"of or relating to cutting (of the womb)"

, cis-, chis-, or scis-

chisel
"an instrument used to cut (into a solid material, such as a wood, stone, or metal)"

scissors
"an cutting instrument (having two blades)"

, and ce- or ci-

cement
"a herd of cutting (stone)"

2

: to strike, beat

3

: to kill, to murder → become descendants like -cide

avicide
"the killing of birds"

deicide
"the killing or killer of a god"

fratricide
"the killing or killer of one's own brother or sister"

4

: to defeat decisively (defeat with heavy losses to the enemy side)
