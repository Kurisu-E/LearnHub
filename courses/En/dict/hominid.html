hominid 1 of 2 noun
hom·​i·​nid ˈhä-mə-nəd -ˌnid
plural hominids

: any of a family (Hominidae) of erect bipedal primate mammals that includes recent humans together with extinct ancestral and related forms and the great apes

Earlier this year, a French team announced the discovery of fossil teeth and limb-bone fragments of a 6-million-year-old hominid ...
— B. Bower

hominid 2 of 2 adjective

: of, relating to, or being a hominid

The discovery of a 3.5-million-year-old hominid skull and other fossil remains in northern Kenya is shaking the human family tree at its very roots.
— Michael Balter
