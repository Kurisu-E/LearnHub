self-deception noun
self-de·​cep·​tion ˌself-di-ˈsep-shən
plural self-deceptions

: the act or an instance of deceiving oneself or the state of being deceived by oneself especially concerning one's true nature, feelings, etc.

the capacity of self-deception

Over time, [Henry] Marsh writes, many doctors start to internalize the stories they tell themselves about their superior judgment and skill. But the best, he adds, unlearn their self-deceptions, and come to accept their fallibility and learn from their mistakes.
— Jennifer Senior
