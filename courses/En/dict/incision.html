incision noun
in·​ci·​sion in-ˈsi-zhən
1
a

: cut, gash

specifically : a wound made especially in surgery by incising the body

2

: an act of incising something

Word History

First Known Use

14th century

Etymology

Old French;

⬅ Old French incision (13c.) "a cutting made in surgery" ← Latin incīsiō (accusative incīsiōnem) "a cutting into," recorded only in figurative senses ⇠ incīs- (past participle stem of incīdere) + -siō "process, action, or result of an action;"

incīdere "to cut, cut through, cut open" ⇠ in- "inward, in, into" + -cidere (sound alternation from caedere "to cut")
