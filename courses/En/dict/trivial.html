Word History

First Known Use

1589

Etymology

Medieval Latin;

⬅ Medieval Latin (5c. to late 15c.) triviālis "found everywhere, commonplace" ⇠ trivi- (stem of trivius/trivium) + -ālis "with intensified meaning;"

trivius "of or belonging to crossroad temples," trivium "crossroads, a place where three roads meet" ⇠ tri- "three" + via "road" + Latin adjective/noun suffix -us/-um

Semantic Change

trivial sense 1 : commonplace, ordinary ← Medieval Latin triviālis "general place" ⇠ Medieval Latin trivius/trivium/trivi- + -ālis "with intensified meaning" ← Medieval Latin trivius/trivium "(of or belonging to) a place where three roads meet" ⇠ tri- "three" + via "road" + Latin adjective/noun suffix -us/-um

trivial sense 2 : generally inconsequential or unimportant ⬅ form is that of Modern English trivial, but its meaning is influenced by the notion or story that people often stop at crossroads to pass the time with inconsequential talk ⬅ general crossroads ⬅ Medieval Latin trivius/trivium "(of or belonging to) a place where three roads meet"

trivial sense 3 : trivial sense 2 specially in mathematics

trivial sense 4 : trivial sense 1 specially in taxonomy
