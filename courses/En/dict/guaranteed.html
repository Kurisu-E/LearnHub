guaranteed adjective
guar·​an·​teed ˌger-ən-ˈtēd ˌgär- ˌga-rən- also ˈger-ən-ˌtēd or ˈgär-ən- ˈga-rən-
1

: assured with a guarantee : protected or promised by a guarantee

a guaranteed annual wage

a guaranteed loan

In some California locales ... public-safety workers can pull down truly swank livings, given their salaries, health benefits and guaranteed pension payouts.
— Amanda Ripley

2

: certain to have a specified result or effect

... the structural imbalance between rich cities and poor countrysides produces economic relations guaranteed to bring out the worst in people.
— Raj Patel

She transforms the discussion of ideas int oa gratuitous spectacle of anger and aggression guaranteed to elevate the Nielsen ratings ...
— Daniel Harris
