Jove noun
ˈjōv
mythology

: Jupiter

Word History

First Known Use

14th century

Etymology

Latin;

⬅ Middle English (1066 to late 15c.) Joves ← New Latin (14c. to the present) Jovis (nominative Juppiter) ← Latin Latin, Medieval Latin (3c. to 15c.) Iovis (nominative Iuppiter)

Related Phrases Containing Jove

Phrases

by Jove

or less commonly

by jove

chiefly British, old-fashioned + sometimes humorous

→ used interjectionally to express surprise or to add emphasis

By jove, Randal, how pleasant a thing is life in London!
— Edward Bulwer-Lytton

"By jove, Lily, you do look a stunner!"
— Edith Wharton

... I've made up my mind to one thing, and, by Jove, I'll stick to it.
— Anthony Trollope

... a setting in which one fully expects to overhear such expletives as Egad! or By Jove! or Bully! ...
— Hudson Bridges
