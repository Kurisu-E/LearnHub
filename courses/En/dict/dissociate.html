dissociate verb
dis·​so·​ci·​ate (ˌ)di-ˈsō-shē-ˌāt
dissociated; dissociating

transitive verb

1

: to separate from association or union with another

tried to dissociate himself from the party's more extreme views

psychology specifically : to mentally detach from one's physical or emotional experiences

attempts to dissociate herself from her past

The director has tried to dissociate herself from her earlier films.

2

: disunite

especially : to subject to chemical dissociation

heat dissociated the ionic bonds

intransitive verb

1

: to undergo dissociation

The compound will dissociate into its constituent ions at high temperatures.

2

: to mutate especially reversibly

The cells began to dissociate under certain conditions, reverting to a more primitive state before reassembling.
