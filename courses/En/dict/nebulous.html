nebulous adjective
neb·​u·​·​lous ˈne-byə-ləs
1

: of, relating to, or resembling a nebula : nebular

2

: indistinct, vague

the plan is too nebulous.

A lot of philosophical concepts can seem nebulous at first, but a good instructor can cut through the jargon and help students see how they apply to day-to-day life.

... this nebulous thing called jazz.
— Josef Woodard

... the nebulous region between mere suspicion and probable cause
— W. R. LaFave & J. H. Israel
